‚ùì Why is a Developer Account Required?

To run WebDriverAgent (WDA) on a physical iOS device, you need to sign the application with a valid Apple Developer certificate. This is a security requirement enforced by iOS - only signed apps can run on real devices.

üì± Simulators: You do NOT need a Developer account to run WDA on iOS Simulators. Simulators don't require code signing.

What the Developer Account provides:

  • Development Team ID - A unique identifier for code signing
  • Signing Certificates - Digital certificates to sign your apps
  • Provisioning Profiles - Permissions to install apps on specific devices

üìù Step-by-Step: Using Free Apple ID

Follow these steps to set up WDA signing with a free Apple ID:

1

Create or Use Existing Apple ID

If you don't have an Apple ID, create one at appleid.apple.com. If you already have one (for iCloud, iPhone, etc.), you can use that.

2

Open Xcode

Launch Xcode on your Mac. You'll need to sign in with your Apple ID.

3

Add Your Apple ID to Xcode

Go to Xcode ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Accounts (or Preferences in older versions). Click the + button and select "Apple ID". Sign in with your credentials.

üì∏ Xcode ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Accounts ‚Üí Add Apple ID
4

Get Your Team ID

After adding your account, click on your Apple ID in the sidebar. You'll see your Personal Team listed. The Team ID is shown in the details or you can find it in the provisioning profile.

For free accounts, your Team ID typically starts with letters and numbers (e.g., A1B2C3D4E5).

5

Open WDA Project in Xcode

Open the WebDriverAgent project in Xcode. It's located at:

~/.appium/node_modules/appium-xcuitest-driver/node_modules/appium-webdriveragent/WebDriverAgent.xcodeproj
6

Configure Signing

In Xcode:

  1. Select "WebDriverAgentRunner" target
  2. Go to "Signing & Capabilities" tab
  3. Check "Automatically manage signing"
  4. Select your Personal Team from the dropdown
  5. If asked, allow Xcode to fix bundle identifier issues
‚öôÔ∏è Xcode ‚Üí WebDriverAgentRunner ‚Üí Signing & Capabilities
7

Trust the Developer on Device

On your iOS device, go to Settings ‚Üí General ‚Üí VPN & Device Management. Find your developer profile and tap "Trust".

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You must trust the developer certificate on the device before WDA can run!

üîç How to Find Your Team ID

Option 1: From Xcode

  1. Open Xcode ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Accounts
  2. Select your Apple ID
  3. Click "Manage Certificates" or view your team details
  4. Your Team ID is displayed in the team information

Option 2: From Apple Developer Portal

  1. Go to developer.apple.com/account
  2. Sign in with your Apple ID
  3. Click on "Membership" in the sidebar
  4. Your Team ID is listed on this page

üìã Team ID Format

A Team ID is a 10-character alphanumeric string like: A1B2C3D4E5

Enter this ID in the WDA Runner's "Development Team ID" field.

üíé Apple Developer Program (Paid)

If you need longer-lasting certificates or are doing continuous integration, you might want a paid account:

  1. Go to developer.apple.com/programs/enroll
  2. Sign in with your Apple ID
  3. Follow the enrollment process
  4. Pay the $99/year fee
  5. Wait for Apple to verify your enrollment (usually 24-48 hours)

üîß Troubleshooting Common Issues

‚ùå "Could not find a valid code signature"

Solution: Make sure you've selected your team in Xcode's Signing & Capabilities, and the bundle identifier is unique.

‚ùå "Untrusted Developer"

Solution: On your iOS device, go to Settings ‚Üí General ‚Üí VPN & Device Management, find your developer profile and tap "Trust".

‚ùå "Maximum 3 devices" error

Solution: Free accounts are limited to 3 devices. Remove unused devices from your Apple ID or upgrade to a paid account.

‚ùå "App requires provisioning profile"

Solution: Ensure "Automatically manage signing" is checked in Xcode and you have a valid Team selected.
